# 401 Content and Marketing — Compliance Safe Content Decision Tree

A one page checklist every LO runs before publishing any borrower-facing content.

---

## Purpose

To build a credible, findable, local presence that helps borrowers and Realtors understand what you do — without tripping Reg Z, RESPA, or fair lending.

## Pick One Content Pillar

Not three. One.

Pick the borrower or partner profile you can talk about for 90 days without running out of material:
- First-time buyers
- Self-employed
- VA / military
- Investor
- Move-up
- Divorce
- Reverse mortgage
- New construction

Then everything you post ties back to that one pillar.

## Five Hook Patterns for Short Video

1. **The myth-buster.** "Most people think you need 20% down. Here's the truth."
2. **The local data drop.** "In [your city], the average home price is up 6% YoY. Here's what that means for you."
3. **The behind-the-scenes.** "Here's what happens after you go under contract on a house."
4. **The mistake.** "The #1 mistake first-time buyers make in [your state]."
5. **The answer.** "Someone asked me last week: should I pay points or take the higher rate?"

## 60 Second Video Template

- **0–5 sec** — Hook. Pattern interrupt. Eye contact. No intro.
- **5–30 sec** — One example. One story. One data point.
- **30–55 sec** — The takeaway. What to do, what to avoid, who to call.
- **55–60 sec** — CTA. "DM me 'yes' if you want the next video."

## The Safe Content Decision Tree

Run every post through this. If any answer is "yes" but you didn't fix it, **do not publish**.

1. **Does it mention a specific rate, payment, down payment dollar amount, or fee dollar amount?**
   - If yes → full Reg Z disclosures required. Send to compliance review.
2. **Does it name a competitor or compare to a named competitor?**
   - If yes → rewrite. No competitor comparisons.
3. **Does it promise or imply a guaranteed outcome?**
   - If yes → rewrite. No "guaranteed approval" or "lowest rate."
4. **Does it include your NMLS ID and Equal Housing logo (where required)?**
   - If no → add before publishing.
5. **Could a reasonable borrower interpret it as advice for their specific situation?**
   - If yes → soften language. "Talk to a licensed loan officer about your options."

## Google Business Profile Build

Complete every field:
- Name, photo, NMLS ID
- Service area
- Hours
- Business description (3 sentences, plain English)
- 5+ photos (headshot, in front of a closing, with partners)
- 5+ reviews from past clients
- Weekly post (every Friday, same time)

## Compliance

- Reg Z triggering terms require full disclosures.
- NMLS ID on every social profile and every post that promotes credit.
- Equal Housing logo where required.
- No superlatives like "best rate" or "guaranteed approval."
- No unverified comparisons to named competitors.

## The Assignment

1. Post 3 short videos this week.
2. Complete your Google Business Profile build.
3. Draft and schedule your monthly newsletter.
4. Run every piece through the safe content decision tree before publishing.

## Behavior Change

LO has a real digital footprint a borrower or Realtor can find on day one of a search.
