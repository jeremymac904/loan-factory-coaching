# 201 Borrower Conversion — First Call Handout

A one page reference for the first borrower call. Print, keep on your desk, review before every consultation.

---

## Purpose

To turn first conversations into clear next steps and completed applications by using a structured, high-trust call format.

## The 43:57 Talk to Listen Ratio

The Golden Ratio. You talk 43% of the time. The borrower talks 57%. If you're talking more than half the call, you're not listening enough.

## High Trust Discovery Questions (15–16 quality)

1. Tell me what brought you in. What's the story?
2. What's your timeline? When are you hoping to be in a home?
3. Are you working with a Realtor yet? Who?
4. Have you done this before? What worked, what didn't?
5. What does your down payment situation look like?
6. Are you looking at any specific neighborhoods or properties?
7. What's most important to you — payment, rate, speed, certainty?
8. What concerns do you have about the process?
9. How's your credit? Any issues in the last 2 years?
10. What's your income — W2, self-employed, both?
11. Any other debts I should know about? Cars, student loans, cards?
12. How long have you been in your current job?
13. Are you planning to stay in this home 5+ years?
14. What would success look like for you at closing?
15. Who else needs to be part of this decision?
16. What questions do you have for me?

## Plan First Language

Never start with rates. Start with the plan.

"Here's what I'd like to do: ask you a few more questions, then put together a clear picture of your options — payment range, the loan amount you qualify for, and the next step. Sound good?"

## Redirecting Rate and Fee Questions

When they ask "what's your rate?":
"That's a fair question. The honest answer is the rate depends on the full picture — credit, down payment, property type, loan amount. Once I have the full picture, I'll bring back your options side by side. Want to keep going?"

Never quote a specific rate or payment in the first call.

## Dated Next Step

Always end the call with a specific, dated next step.

"Let's plan to reconnect Wednesday at 2 p.m. I'll have your pre-qual letter and a clear picture of your options. Does that work?"

Not "I'll follow up." Not "Let me think about it." A date, a time, a deliverable.

## The Assignment

1. Run one first call using this structure.
2. Track your talk-to-listen ratio (estimate if needed).
3. Note your 5 best questions and your 5 worst.
4. Set a dated next step on every call.

## Compliance

- No teaser rates in any borrower facing artifact.
- No promises about closing dates or guarantees.
- Reg Z triggering terms list reviewed before any printed or posted material.

## Behavior Change

LO talk to listen ratio moves toward the 43:57 golden ratio. Question quality improves week over week.
